Solar Power Calculator

This page is dedicated to ensuring that all users have a clear understanding of all main solar panel terminology. This ensures that you are able to make a clear assessment of the meaning of results without worrying that you didn't understand all of the various inputs.

Term
Definition
Watt (W) A unit of power. A typical light bulb uses 60 watts of power.
Kilowatt (kW) One thousand watts.
Solar Panel A panel designed to absorb the sun's rays as a source of energy for generating electricity or heating.
Solar Power The power obtained from a solar panel.
Orientation The outward facing direction of the panels. E.g. North, South-East etc.
Off-Grid Solar A home which is entirely supported by their own solar panels.
Kilowatt-hour (kWh) One thousand watts generated in one hour. E.g. An average household uses 10,000kWh per year.
Inverter A device which converts the power from solar panels from DC (direct current) to AC (alternating current) for use in the home.
Grid-Connected Panel A panel which generates power for the power grid.
Grid The system of electrical transmission of power through distribution lines.
Array/System A group of solar panels wired together to act as a single unit.
Tilt Angle The angle of inclination between the panel and the horizontal ground level.
Electricity Cost The cost of electricity (per kW). This can be found on a power bill, or by looking up your electrical plan.
Feed-In Tariff A fixed value, used as an incentive for purchasing panels. Usually gives a better electricity cost for feeding back in to the grid than would be expected.
